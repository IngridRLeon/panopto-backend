Adaptive Smart Study
System

Every session is bespoke — generated fresh from what the learner needs most, getting smarter with every round.

Panopto Folio · V1
Core principles
⚡
Always on, no generate step
CTAs are always present. Tap to start immediately — no naming, no saving, no library to manage.
🎯
Always 10 to start
Every session opens with exactly 10 cards or questions. No configuration. Just study.
🔄
Infinite keep-going loop
After each 10-card batch, choose to keep going. Every round is smarter than the last.
✨
Fresh phrasing every time
Weak KTs are revisited with new wording — not the same card repeated, but a fresh approach.
Session flow
1
Learner taps Flashcards or Quiz
CTAs are permanently visible at every level. No generate step. No modal on first tap — unless at Folio or Study Set level (see below).
2
Content picker (Folio & Study Set only)
A lightweight checklist appears — all Learning Materials pre-checked. Learner can deselect any they want to skip. Hitting Start with everything checked is identical to auto-start.
Skipped at Learning Material & KT level — starts immediately
3
10 adaptive cards generated
The system selects KTs from the pool using weighted random sampling. Never-seen KTs and low-accuracy KTs have the highest weight. Cards are freshly worded per KT and attempt number.
4
Study & self-grade
Card flip, Got it / Missed it. KT performance records update after every card — not just at session end.
5
Results + confidence check-in
Accuracy gauge, missed KT list, 1–5 confidence rating. Low confidence boosts those KTs' weight in the next round.
∞
Keep going — or finish
Two options: Keep going (10 more, smarter) or I'm done for now. The loop is infinite. A running tally shows total concepts studied this sitting.

A diagnostic, not just a visual

Mind maps remain static in V1 — they always show all KTs in scope. But KTs that need review are flagged with an amber highlight, turning the mind map into a useful study diagnostic at a glance.

KTs flagged as "needs review" are those with below 70% accuracy, or a confidence rating of 1–2. KTs never studied are not flagged — the system only flags based on evidence.
